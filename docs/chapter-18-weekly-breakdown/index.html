Chapter 15: Weekly Breakdown (Weeks 1–13)

Overview​

This chapter provides a structured 13-week learning path designed to guide students through the comprehensive study of Physical AI and humanoid robotics. Each week builds upon the previous week's concepts, creating a progressive learning experience that moves from foundational concepts to advanced applications.

Weekly Structure​

Week 1: Introduction to Physical AI​

  • Understanding the concept of Physical AI
  • Exploring embodied intelligence principles
  • Introduction to sensorimotor learning
  • Overview of the course structure and learning objectives

Week 2: Humanoid Robotics Fundamentals​

  • Components of humanoid robots
  • Kinematic structures and degrees of freedom
  • Actuation systems and control
  • Sensing systems for humanoid robots

Week 3: Sensorimotor Learning and Embodied Intelligence​

  • Principles of sensorimotor learning
  • Embodied cognition theory
  • Learning through interaction
  • Environmental affordances

Week 4: ROS 2 Architecture and Communication​

  • ROS 2 nodes and communication patterns
  • Topics, services, and actions
  • Quality of Service (QoS) policies
  • Package and workspace organization

Week 5: Advanced ROS 2 Concepts​

  • Lifecycle nodes and state management
  • Launch systems and configuration
  • Parameter management
  • Real-time considerations in ROS 2

Week 6: URDF and Robot Modeling​

  • Unified Robot Description Format (URDF)
  • Links, joints, and kinematic chains
  • Xacro macros and modular design
  • Robot State Publisher

Week 7: Simulation Environments - Part 1​

  • Introduction to simulation in robotics
  • Gazebo simulation environment
  • Physics engines and collision detection
  • Sensor simulation

Week 8: Simulation Environments - Part 2​

  • Unity robotics simulation
  • NVIDIA Isaac Sim overview
  • Digital twin concepts
  • Simulation-to-reality transfer

Week 9: NVIDIA Isaac Platform - Perception​

  • Perception systems in NVIDIA Isaac
  • Computer vision integration
  • 3D perception and scene understanding
  • Sensor fusion in simulation

Week 10: NVIDIA Isaac Platform - Navigation​

  • Visual Simultaneous Localization and Mapping (VSLAM)
  • Navigation 2 (Nav2) in simulation
  • Path planning and obstacle avoidance
  • Localization and mapping algorithms

Week 11: NVIDIA Isaac Platform - Reinforcement Learning​

  • Reinforcement learning basics
  • Training agents in simulation
  • Reward functions and environments
  • Transfer learning concepts

Week 12: Humanoid Robotics - Kinematics and Control​

  • Forward and inverse kinematics
  • Walking and locomotion patterns
  • Manipulation and grasping
  • Balance and stability control

Week 13: Capstone Integration and Future Directions​

  • Integration of all concepts learned
  • Vision-Language-Action systems
  • Research frontiers in Physical AI
  • Future directions and career paths

Learning Progression​

Each week includes:

  • Theoretical foundations: Core concepts and principles
  • Conceptual examples: Illustrative scenarios without executable code
  • Cross-references: Connections to previous and future concepts
  • Self-assessment: Questions to validate understanding

Assessment Structure​

Weekly Assessments​

  • Multiple choice questions on key concepts
  • Conceptual problem solving
  • Cross-chapter connections
  • Application scenarios

Mid-term Evaluation (Week 6-7)​

  • Comprehensive understanding of ROS 2 and simulation concepts
  • Ability to explain system integration
  • Analysis of simulation-to-reality challenges

Final Assessment (Week 13)​

  • Synthesis of all concepts learned
  • Critical analysis of Physical AI applications
  • Future research directions and challenges

Prerequisites and Dependencies​

Each week builds on previous concepts:

  • Weeks 1-3: Foundation for all subsequent learning
  • Week 4: Prerequisite for Weeks 5-6 and all ROS 2 applications
  • Week 6: Required for simulation applications in Weeks 7-12
  • Weeks 7-10: Foundation for advanced applications in Weeks 11-13

Flexible Learning Paths​

While the 13-week structure provides a comprehensive path, students may choose alternative paths:

  • ROS 2 focused: Weeks 1-6, 13
  • Simulation focused: Weeks 1-3, 7-10, 13
  • Humanoid focused: Weeks 1-3, 6, 11-13

Resources and Support​

Each week includes references to official documentation, academic papers, and additional reading materials to support deeper learning for interested students.
