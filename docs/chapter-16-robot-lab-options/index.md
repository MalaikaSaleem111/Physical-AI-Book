# Chapter 16: Robot Lab Options (Unitree Go2/G1/H1 comparison)

## Overview

Setting up a robotics laboratory requires careful consideration of available platforms, capabilities, and research objectives. This chapter provides a comparative analysis of popular humanoid and quadrupedal robotic platforms, focusing on Unitree's offerings and other comparable systems. The analysis emphasizes theoretical understanding of platform capabilities rather than specific setup instructions.

## Platform Selection Criteria

### Research Objectives

#### Academic Research Focus
- **Fundamental Research**: Platforms suitable for basic research
- **Applied Research**: Platforms for practical applications
- **Educational Use**: Platforms for teaching and learning
- **Benchmarking**: Platforms for standardized evaluation

#### Technical Requirements
- **Degrees of Freedom**: Number and configuration of joints
- **Sensing Capabilities**: Available sensors and their specifications
- **Computing Power**: On-board processing capabilities
- **Development Environment**: Software support and tools

### Cost Considerations

#### Initial Investment
- **Platform Cost**: Base price of the robotic platform
- **Accessories**: Additional sensors, batteries, and peripherals
- **Infrastructure**: Required safety equipment and workspace
- **Shipping and Import**: International shipping considerations

#### Ongoing Costs
- **Maintenance**: Regular maintenance and calibration
- **Consumables**: Batteries, replacement parts, etc.
- **Software Licenses**: Proprietary software requirements
- **Training**: Personnel training and certification

## Unitree Platform Analysis

### Unitree H1 Humanoid Robot

#### Specifications Overview
- **Degrees of Freedom**: 25+ joints for full-body control
- **Height**: Approximately 1.3 meters
- **Weight**: Around 40-45 kg
- **Battery Life**: Several hours of operation
- **Payload Capacity**: Ability to carry additional sensors

#### Technical Capabilities
- **Locomotion**: Bipedal walking, running, and balance
- **Manipulation**: Basic manipulation capabilities
- **Sensing**: Integrated cameras, IMU, and other sensors
- **Control**: Advanced control algorithms for dynamic movement

#### Research Applications
- **Humanoid Locomotion**: Studying bipedal walking and balance
- **Human-Robot Interaction**: Research in social robotics
- **Manipulation Studies**: Basic manipulation research
- **Perception Systems**: Testing perception in dynamic environments

### Unitree G1 Humanoid Robot

#### Specifications Overview
- **Degrees of Freedom**: Advanced joint configuration
- **Design Philosophy**: Focus on cost-effective humanoid research
- **Sensing**: Integrated sensor suite for research
- **Development Platform**: Support for ROS and other frameworks

#### Technical Capabilities
- **Mobility**: Walking and basic locomotion
- **Interactivity**: Designed for human interaction studies
- **Expandability**: Modular design for research modifications
- **Safety Features**: Built-in safety mechanisms

#### Research Applications
- **Humanoid Robotics**: Fundamental humanoid research
- **AI Integration**: Testing AI algorithms on physical platforms
- **Educational Use**: Teaching platform for robotics concepts
- **Social Robotics**: Human interaction and social behavior

### Unitree Go2 Quadruped Robot

#### Specifications Overview
- **Degrees of Freedom**: 12 joints (3 per leg)
- **Mobility**: Quadrupedal locomotion with dynamic gaits
- **Speed**: Various gaits from walking to running
- **Terrain Adaptability**: Navigation on various surfaces

#### Technical Capabilities
- **Locomotion**: Advanced quadrupedal control algorithms
- **Balance**: Dynamic balance and recovery behaviors
- **Sensing**: Integrated perception systems
- **Autonomy**: Autonomous navigation capabilities

#### Research Applications
- **Quadrupedal Locomotion**: Studying four-legged movement
- **Terrain Navigation**: Research in rough terrain navigation
- **Dynamic Control**: Advanced control algorithm testing
- **Search and Rescue**: Applications in challenging environments
